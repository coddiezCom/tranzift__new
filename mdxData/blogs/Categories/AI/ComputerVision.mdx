### What is Computer Vision?

Computer Vision is a field of AI that focuses on developing algorithms and techniques to enable computers to interpret and understand visual information from the world around them. By analyzing and processing images and videos, Computer Vision systems can identify objects, recognize patterns, detect anomalies, and extract valuable insights that can be used to enhance decision-making, improve efficiency, and create immersive user experiences.

### Key Components of Computer Vision

- **Image Acquisition**: Capturing and acquiring high-quality images or videos using cameras or sensors.
- **Image Preprocessing**: Cleaning, enhancing, and preparing visual data for analysis.
- **Feature Extraction**: Identifying and extracting important features or patterns from images.
- **Object Detection and Recognition**: Identifying and classifying objects within images or videos.
- **Scene Understanding**: Analyzing and interpreting complex scenes, contexts, and relationships between objects.

### Applications of Computer Vision

Computer Vision has a wide range of applications across various industries, including:

- **Automotive**: Autonomous vehicles, driver assistance systems, and traffic management.
- **Healthcare**: Medical imaging, disease diagnosis, and surgical assistance.
- **Retail**: Inventory management, product recognition, and customer behavior analysis.
- **Manufacturing**: Quality control, defect detection, and process automation.
- **Security and Surveillance**: Facial recognition, anomaly detection, and threat monitoring.
